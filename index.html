Hi, it's great to meet you.

I don't get to see a lot of people around here.
How much exactly? No tengo Google Analytics (that's also my cookie policy).
Who am I, you ask? Wow, you must be really lost.
Allow me to introduce myself. My name's Karel.

I'm eager to learn.

"You should learn more" is what my Dad always said. He was right. I decided to give my web skills an upgrade.
I started out small. Codecademy, FreeCodeCamp ... Those kind of things.

But that got boring pretty quickly and Udemy was my next stop. Some examples of courses I followed:

  • The Web Developer Bootcamp - This was the first paid course I bought on Udemy. And you can learn so much from it: jQuery, Express, REST (hang that table above your bed), Mongoose ... It's worth its weight in gold!
  • Object-oriented programming in Javascript - Spoiler: small course with little practical stuff here but Mosh is just such a good instructor. Excellent course for everyone interested in Javascript!

Then I spent some time learning the basics of SQL and MongoDB through books and experimentation. Around that time, I decided to get serious with development.

So I'd set my eyes on combining my day job with a C# course of 2 years. But the day I went to sign up was a disaster. The organisation did not yet have a formal decision on the schedule, the lecturer was not present and it was unsure if the course would reach the required number of participants. I left with a huge feeling of disappointment.

The next day I woke up and told myself that I would find another course that sparked my interest. And there it was: Blockchain Developer & Architect. It had "developer" in the title so I was sold! At the time of writing I have almost completed the 1,5 year curriculum. A sneak preview of course content:

  • Hashing and encryption algorithms and how they work under the hood.
  • How to set up a Hyperledger Fabric network. If you're looking for detailed articles about Hyperledger, Ledger Tech is where you need to be!
  • Creating a web application that interacts with the Ethereum network through web3.js and Metamask.

Building stuff.
